# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Three tiers, one problem: not showing up when AI recommends.

- **Franchise operators and multi-location brands**: 5–500+ locations, each needing consistent AI visibility signals. The pain is scale — one bad listing or inconsistent NAP can suppress an entire region. Decision-makers are regional marketing managers and franchise development officers.
- **Enterprise and mid-market companies**: Established brands investing in AI search as a channel alongside traditional SEO and paid. Marketing directors and CMOs who want measurable citation share and category authority. Budget is not the friction — proof is.
- **Growth-stage SMBs**: $1M–$10M revenue businesses that want to compete against larger players by owning AI recommendations in their category before those players do. Lean on the first-mover window. Owners and operators who understand results but not the technical mechanism.

All three are skeptical of marketing agencies and wary of vague promises. They need to see proof before they buy.

## Product Purpose

Shark AI Solutions is an AI visibility consulting agency serving SMBs, multi-location franchises, and enterprise brands. It does two things: (1) get businesses appearing in AI-generated recommendations by fixing their signals, structured data, reviews, and content at scale; (2) deploy AI employees (chat, voice, CRM, reputation, sales) to automate lead follow-up and operations 24/7 across locations. The homepage exists to turn a skeptical decision-maker — franchise operator, CMO, or growth-stage owner — into a booked consultation. Success = a submitted audit request or a phone call.

## Brand Personality

Authoritative · Approachable · Proven

Voice: direct, plain-language, no jargon unless immediately explained. Tone of a trusted expert — sharp enough for an enterprise CMO, clear enough for a franchise operator who doesn't care how it works, only that it does. Confidence without arrogance. Results-first.

Emotional goals: make the visitor feel (1) understood — "yes, this is the problem I have at my scale"; (2) trusted — "this company has done it for businesses like mine and can prove it"; (3) urgency — "AI search is being carved up right now and first-movers win."

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS / startup**: No blue-purple gradients, hero metrics ("10x your ROI"), rocket ship imagery, or "scale your business" copyspeak. No identical icon grids. No glassmorphism as decoration.
- **Corporate marketing agency**: No navy + gold palette, stock photos of handshakes, buzzword-dense decks, or vague "strategy and execution" positioning.
- **Cheap local biz site**: No clip art, busy cluttered layouts, small-town directory aesthetic, or DIY builder look.
- **AI template output**: No eyebrow on every section, no numbered section markers used as decoration, no identical card grids repeating endlessly, no gradient text.

## Design Principles

1. **Results over promises.** Lead with documented outcomes — Page 3 → #2, #4 → #1 Realtor, 96% member gap closed. Specificity is the proof. Never let a section make a claim that isn't backed by a named result or a concrete mechanism.
2. **Apple restraint, local roots.** The visual clarity of Apple.com — white space, confident typography, deliberate color — applied to a Tampa Bay business, not a faceless tech company. Let the offer speak. Nothing decorative that doesn't serve comprehension.
3. **Teach, then sell.** The visitor needs to understand the AI shift before they can value the service. Educate clearly, then make the ask. Don't open with price or pitch.
4. **Expert confidence.** State facts plainly. "AI recommends 1–3 businesses. If you're not in that shortlist, you don't exist to that buyer." No hedging, no softening, no qualifications. The expert voice doesn't apologize.
5. **Every section earns its place.** No section, element, or decoration exists for visual variety. If it doesn't move the visitor toward an audit request, it's cut.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG level required. All text must be clearly readable for general audiences (no muted-gray-on-near-white). Keyboard navigation must be fully functional. Every interactive element needs a visible focus state. Reduced motion must be respected for all animations.
